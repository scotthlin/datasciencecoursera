The purpose of this project is to simulate 1000 times the mean of 40 exponential distribution values in R to test the CLT and its associated proofs

##Q1: Show the sample mean and compare it to the theoretical mean of the distribution
Below, well first check out the sample mean. Well show the histogram of values. Because its not a normal distribution, its hard to see where the mean is. Ill print the mean after the graph.
r expdist <- rexp(1000,0.2); hist(expdist, breaks = 20, main = "Histogram of 1000 Exp Values", xlab = "Exp Values")
## [1] 5.141518
Then well check the distribution of the means of 40 exponential values, running the simulation 1000 times
r nosim = 1000; n = 40; lambda = 0.2; mean_values = NULL for(i in 1:nosim) mean_values = c(mean_values, mean(rexp(n,lambda))) hist(mean_values, breaks = 40, main = "Histogram of Mean Values", xlab = "Mean Exp Values")
The mean calculated in the first graph should compare favorably to bump in the second graph, showing that the mean of the sample should be the same as the mean of the theoretical mean of the distribution.

2. Show how variable the sample is (via variance) and compare it to the theoretical variance of the distribution

The theoretical variance of the distribution of means should be the squared of the standard error of the sample, which is just the variance divided by n squared.

expdist <- var(rexp(1000,0.2)); mndisvar <- expdist/1000^2
